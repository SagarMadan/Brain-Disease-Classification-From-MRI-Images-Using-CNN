Brief about project

Brain diseases including various forms of Dementia, pose significant challenges to healthcare systems worldwide. Early and accurate diagnosis of these conditions is crucial for effective treatment and management. Magnetic Resonance Imaging (MRI) is a powerful tool for assessing brain structure and detecting abnormalities associated with neurological disorders. In recent years, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) have emerged as a promising approach for automatic feature extraction and classification in medical image analysis tasks. This study explores the application of CNNs for brain disease classification from MRI images, focusing on distinguishing between " Mild Demented ", " Non-Demented ", and " Very Mild Demented " states.
